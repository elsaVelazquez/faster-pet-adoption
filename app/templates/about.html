Introduction

How can we get pets adopted faster?

Purpose

I seek a position as a Junior Data Scientist, specializing in ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) pipelines.

Method

I evaluated the entire approach to displaying a rescue dog online and recommend that during this pandemic, shelters focus on writing solid text descriptions of the dog over pouring resources into photos, despite age, breed, color or dog size.

About

Who

I am Elsa Velazquez and you can find out more about me by clicking on my LinkedIn, my GitHub, or elsa-data-sci.tech/home.

I invented this project because I love building data pipelines and because as a disabled veteran, I have gotten a second chance in life thanks to my service dogs and feel I am fulfilling my life mission to bring dog-kind closer to human-kind.

The animal shelter data is scraped from PetFinders.com and full twitter dataset is from https://pythonprogramming.net/static/downloads/short_reviews/

What

This project is based on the MultiNomial Naive Bayes (MNB) algorithm, and on Natural Language Processing (NLP).

I used math to check the impact of a dog's online presence and determined that during this pandemic (from start date to stop date), the human-input description of the dog matters most, and the dog's picture does not matter (click here to find out more).

Illustrated below is the theory behind MNB as it applies to this classification project.

Multonimial Naive Bayes Representation

The text portion of this project is based on the TF-IDf score, which is a part of NLP.

Essentially, this is a mathematical way of putting words into numbers so we can extract meaningful information.Click here to read more.

The combination of MNB and TF-IDF allowed me to see which words made the biggest impact on the dog's online presence.

Because we see that there is really a difference in the words used to create what I classified as a positive sentiment in dogs, which is distinct from words used to describe dogs that were not yet adopted, my theory that the text matters more than the images still persists.

Why

My predictions can't be based on accuracy scores because my dataset has many more adopted pets than adoptable pets. I therefore used an F1 score of 83%, which is 33% higher than a prediction based on chance.

The prediction is our classification of the input text describing the dog.

To show a more likely scenario of the sentiment invoked by the dog descriptions, I am simultaneously classifying the input description against a well-known, vetted, Twitter Airlines sentiment analysis dataset.

Were it not for the pandemic, it is very likely that the input data would be classified in a sentimnet very much like the Twitter result.
